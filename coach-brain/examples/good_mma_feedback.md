# Good MMA Feedback Examples

Note how every correction accounts for the transition threat — no pure-boxing or pure-BJJ advice.

---

**Coach's Read:** "You won the striking in space and lost the fight at the fence. Every exchange ended with a straight-back exit, the cage found you twice, and the takedown came the second time — off the underhook you conceded while covering up."

**Adjustment (technical):** "Your stance squares up when they feint the level change — that offers both legs. Keep the stagger and answer the feint with a hip check and frames, not with a stance change."

**Adjustment (tactical):** "Angle off after the last strike of every combination. Their whole game starts when your back finds the fence — deny the wall and you deny the wrestling."

**Adjustment (habit / drill):** "Wall-escape rounds: start every round with your back on the cage and an underhook conceded. Frame the face, pummel the underhook back, circle out. Getting taken down or staying pinned 30 seconds resets it."

**Quick cues:** "Angle off — don't find the fence." / "Jab before you shoot." / "Underhook or leave the wall."

**Caution note pattern:** "The ground exchange from 1:10 is a tangle — I can read posture and who's driving, not the specific grips, so the read stays at the position level. The cage is out of frame after 1:20, so I won't call wall position there."
